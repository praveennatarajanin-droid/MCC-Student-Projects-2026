Exp 01 Cation Test

Salt Analysis

Colour Observation & Precipitate Test

Cu²⁺ + 2OH⁻ → Cu(OH)₂↓ (blue)
Fe³⁺ + 3OH⁻ → Fe(OH)₃↓ (brown)
Dissolve an unknown salt and add NaOH, NH₃, HCl or H₂SO₄. Identify the metal cation from the colour of the precipitate formed.
1Select unknown salt sample
2Dissolve in distilled water
3Add diagnostic reagent drop-wise
4Observe precipitate colour
5Identify the metal cation
Cu²⁺ Ni²⁺ Co²⁺ Mn²⁺ Fe³⁺

Exp 02 Flame Test
🔥

Flame Test

Conc. HCl Paste Method (Bunsen Burner)

M⁺ + energy → M⁺* → M⁺ + hν
Characteristic emission spectrum
Form a paste of unknown substance with conc. HCl on a wire loop. Hold in the non-luminous Bunsen flame and record the characteristic colour.
1Select unknown substance
2Add 1–2 drops conc. HCl
3Make paste on wire loop
4Hold in Bunsen flame
5Record flame colour
B³⁺ Cu²⁺ Sr²⁺ Ca²⁺ Na⁺ K⁺
⏱ ~8 min 📋 100 marks
Exp 03 Anion Test
🧫

Carbonate Test

Dilute HCl + Limewater Method

CO₃²⁻ + 2HCl → CO₂↑ + H₂O + 2Cl⁻
CO₂ + Ca(OH)₂ → CaCO₃↓ + H₂O
Add dilute HCl to the substance on a watch glass. Brisk effervescence with colourless gas that turns limewater milky confirms CO₃²⁻.
1Place substance on watch glass
2Add drops of dilute HCl
3Observe effervescence & gas
4Pass gas through limewater
5Confirm carbonate ion present
CO₃²⁻ HCO₃⁻ S²⁻ SO₃²⁻ NO₂⁻
⏱ ~8 min 📋 100 marks
Exp 04 Dry Test
🔥

Action of Heat

Dry Heating Test in Test Tube

Zn²⁺ (white) + Δ → ZnO (yellow hot)
NH₄⁺ + Δ → NH₃↑ (pungent smelling)
Heat a small quantity of an unknown mixture in a dry test tube. Observe physical color changes and evolved gases, then confirm with chemical tests.
1Select mixture sample
2Place in a clean, dry test tube
3Heat over Bunsen burner flame
4Observe color change & gas evolved
5Conduct confirmatory gas tests
CO₃²⁻ NO₃⁻ NH₄⁺ Zn²⁺
⏱ ~10 min 📋 100 marks
Exp 05 Acid Test
⚗️

Acid Vapour Tests

Concentrated Sulphuric Acid Test

NaCl + H₂SO₄ → NaHSO₄ + HCl↑ (white fumes with NH₃)
2NaBr + 2H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + SO₂↑ + Br₂↑ (red fumes)
Warm a small quantity of the substance with Concentrated Sulphuric Acid. Observe evolved gases and perform confirmatory tests for Chloride, Bromide, and Fluoride.
1Select unknown mixture sample
2Add Conc. sulphuric acid using dropper
3Warm carefully over Bunsen burner
4Perform confirmatory gas/vapour tests
5Identify the active anions present
Cl⁻ Br⁻ F⁻
⏱ ~10 min 📋 100 marks
Exp 06 MnO₂ Test
🔬

MnO₂ Test

Manganese Dioxide + Con. Sulphuric Acid

MnO₂ + 2NaCl + 2H₂SO₄ → Cl₂↑ (greenish-yellow)
MnO₂ + 2KBr + 2H₂SO₄ → Br₂↑ (reddish-brown)
Add equal amounts of MnO₂ and a few drops of Concentrated Sulphuric Acid to the mixture. Warm if necessary. Identify Chloride, Bromide, and Oxalate ions from evolved gas.
1Select unknown mixture sample
2Add equal amounts of MnO₂ powder
3Add a few drops of Con. H₂SO₄
4Warm & observe gas/vapour evolved
5Test with starch iodide, fluorescence paper & limewater
Cl⁻ Br⁻ C₂O₄²⁻
⏱ ~10 min 📋 100 marks
Exp 07 Copper Test
🧪

Copper Turnings Test

Mixture + Copper Turnings + Con. H₂SO₄

KNO₃ + H₂SO₄ → KHSO₄ + HNO₃
4HNO₃ + Cu → Cu(NO₃)₂ (green) + 2NO₂↑ (brown) + 2H₂O
Add a piece of copper turnings and a few drops of Concentrated Sulphuric Acid to the mixture. Warm and observe the solution turning green and evolution of reddish-brown vapours.
1Select unknown mixture sample
2Add a piece of copper turnings
3Add Concentrated Sulphuric Acid
4Warm gently over Bunsen flame
5Perform confirmatory gas tests
NO₃⁻ Cl⁻ Br⁻
⏱ ~10 min 📋 100 marks
